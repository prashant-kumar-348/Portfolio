Hostage X

Gameplay Programmer • 2025

Unreal Engine Blender Blueprint

Project Overview

This project is a first-person shooter (FPS) game developed in Unreal Engine 5 using Blueprints. The game centers around a tense hostage-rescue scenario inside a terrorist-occupied house. A hostage is locked inside a secured room, and the key required to unlock the door spawns at a random location in the environment each time the game begins. The player must explore the house, eliminate all terrorists, and locate the randomly generated key to free the hostage and complete the mission. The gameplay combines fast-paced combat, AI-driven enemy behavior, and environmental exploration, creating a dynamic and replayable experience.

My Contributions

  • Game design & core mechanics – Designed the hostage-rescue concept, random key system, and mission structure.
  • Blueprint scripting – Implemented all gameplay logic, player systems, AI behavior, damage systems, HUD updates, and win/lose conditions using Unreal Engine Blueprints.
  • Level design – Created and arranged the interior environment, room layouts, item placements, and enemy positions.
  • AI design – Built enemy movement, detection, patrol, and combat behaviors using blueprint logic.
  • Combat systems – Implemented weapon mechanics, firing logic, damage calculation, and enemy reactions.
  • UI/HUD – Created player health display, key notification, mission updates, and end-game screens.

  • Implemented a timed health regeneration system where the player regains health after avoiding damage for a short period.

Technical Implementation

AI Systems

  • Enemy Patrol System: Terrorists patrol predefined paths or hold defensive positions inside the house.
  • Detection & Combat Behavior: Enemies detect the player based on line-of-sight and distance checks. Once engaged, they chase and shoot at the player using Blueprint logic. Enemies stop, aim, and fire with adjustable accuracy and firing intervals.
  • Damage & Death Handling: Enemies react to hits, take damage, and die with proper animations and cleanup events. Kill count updates automatically to check end-game conditions.
  • Behavior Implementation: Built entirely using Blueprints, behavior trees, and Unreal’s AI Controller system.

Weapon Framework

  • Player Weapon: Implemented hitscan shooting (line trace) for bullets. Added muzzle flash, firing sound, and impact effects. Blueprint-controlled ammo, firing rate, and recoil behavior.
  • Damage System: Line trace detects enemy hit → applies damage event. Enemies and player both use health variables and death triggers.
  • Interaction: Shooting interacts with destructible or interactive objects when needed.
  • Added a player health regeneration mechanic that activates after a configurable delay once the player stops taking damage.
  • Implemented smooth gradual healing using timers and event-driven Blueprint logic.

Performance

  • Optimized Blueprints by reducing unnecessary tick functions.
  • Used event-driven systems for AI, key spawn, and door interactions.
  • Simplified collision settings to minimize physics and CPU load.
  • LODs (Level of Detail) used for props to maintain good framerate.
  • Cleaned unused references and improved Blueprint organization for faster runtime.
  • Ensured smooth movement for AI and stable FPS during combat sequences.
  • Optimized health regeneration using timers instead of Tick to avoid unnecessary performance overhead.
